Historical Timeline

Key moments in the evolution of Pavia's defensive structures

1st Century BC

Roman Foundations

The first defensive walls were constructed around the ancient Roman settlement of Ticinum (Pavia), marking the foundation of the cityâ€™s original boundaries along the banks of the Ticino River. These early fortifications not only defined the urban layout but also symbolized the strategic and military significance of the city in protecting trade routes and Roman interests in northern Italy..

6th Century

Lombard Capital

As the capital of the Lombard Kingdom, Pavia underwent significant fortification works that transformed its defensive landscape. The city walls were reinforced with new towers and extended to encompass emerging districts, reflecting both its growing population and its political importance as a seat of royal power and administration.

12th Century

Medieval Expansion

A subsequent wall, constructed between the mid-11th and early 12th centuries, denoted a phase of medieval fortification, aligning with the city's evolving needs.During this period, a new circuit of walls was constructed, expanding far beyond the limits of the ancient Roman enclosure.

16th Century

Spanish Bastions

During the Spanish domination of the Duchy of Milan, the Spanish walls was established, closely mirroring the trajectory of the preceding third wall. These Spanish walls were modernized with bastions to withstand artillery, though some medieval gates were demolished.This transformation reflects the geopolitical shifts and military influences during the Spanish dominion.
