A City Fortified Through Centuries

Pavia's walls and gates tell a story of power, defense, and urban evolution from Roman times through the Middle Ages.

From Roman Foundations to Medieval Majesty

The first walls of Pavia date back to Roman times when the city was known as Ticinum. These early fortifications were expanded and reinforced over centuries as the city grew in strategic importance.

By the Middle Ages, Pavia boasted an impressive circuit of walls with numerous towers and gates, protecting its status as a key Lombard capital and later as a free commune.

Historical Timeline

Key moments in the evolution of Pavia's defensive structures

1st Century BC

Roman Foundations

First defensive walls built around Ticinum (Roman Pavia), establishing the city's original footprint along the Ticino River.

6th Century

Lombard Capital

As capital of the Lombard Kingdom, Pavia's walls were strengthened with new towers and expanded to protect the growing city.

12th Century

Medieval Expansion

The free commune of Pavia constructs new walls encompassing suburbs, with seven main gates controlling access to the city.

16th Century

Spanish Modifications

Under Spanish rule, walls were modernized with bastions to withstand artillery, though some medieval gates were demolished.
